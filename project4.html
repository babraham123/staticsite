Project: Accelerating Cylinders

Completion Date: November 2012

Project: Simulation of unsteady flow over a 2D cylinder

Description

     Impulsively started and accelerating two-dimensional flows around a circular cylinder are simulated with the immersed boundary projection method. This study investigates the flow dynamics of acceleration at low Reynolds numbers, Re = O(100)-O(1000). The evolution of drag and wake geometry are examined in detail to provide insights into the effect of different velocity profiles. We observe a strong dependent on β, the power of acceleration, and on the reference Reynolds number. In addition, travel distance is found to be an effective time scale in collapsing drag and wake length.

Technical Skills

     We had to manage hundreds of gigabytes worth of data and to algorithmically extract information about the wake geometry from large vector fields.
